Why is this important?

Rashes are common in pediatric patients. The ability to generate a differential diagnosis and formulate an assessment and plan for a patient presenting with a rash , taking into consideration the characteristics of the patient (i.e. age), the rash, and associated signs and symptoms, is essential to pediatric practice. Specific domain objectives:

• Obtain a focused history and physical exam and generate a differential diagnosis for a patients presenting with a rash.

• Describe the epidemiology, clinical presentation, evaluation, and management for each of the following diagnoses: Viral rash (e.g. roseola, measles, coxsackie), scarlet fever, eczema, urticaria, contact dermatitis, thrush, atopic dermatitis, seborrheic dermatitis, acne, molluscum contagiosum, tinea corporis
