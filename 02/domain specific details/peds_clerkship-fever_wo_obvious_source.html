Why is this important?

Fever is a very common presenting complaint in pediatric patients. The ability to generate a differential diagnosis and formulate an assessment and plan for a patient presenting with fever without an obvious source is essential to pediatric practice.

Specific domain objectives:

• Obtain a focused history and physical exam and generate a differential diagnosis for a patient of different age groups presenting with fever without an obvious source.

• Describe the epidemiology, clinical presentation, evaluation, and management for each of the following diagnoses: Sepsis, occult bacteremia, urinary tract infection, systemic viral infection (e.g. EBV), autoimmune diseases (e.g. JIA), malignancy (e.g. ALL)
