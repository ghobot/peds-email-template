Why is this important?

Neurologic symptoms are common presenting complaints in pediatric patients and potentially life-threatening. The ability to generate a differential diagnosis and formulate an assessment and plan for a patient presenting with neurologic symptoms, taking into consideration the characteristics of the patient (i.e. age) and the illness, is essential to pediatric practice.

Specific domain objectives:

• Obtain a focused history and physical exam and generate a differential diagnosis for a patients presenting with each of the following symptoms: Lethargy, irritability, fussiness, headache, loss of consciousness, seizures

• Describe the epidemiology, clinical presentation, evaluation, and management for each of the following diagnoses: Meningitis, concussion, febrile seizure, brain tumor, closed head injury, tension headache
