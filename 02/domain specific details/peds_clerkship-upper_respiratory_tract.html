Why is this important?

Upper respiratory symptoms are common presenting complaints in pediatric patients. The ability to generate a differential diagnosis and formulate an assessment and plan for a patient presenting with upper respiratory symptoms, taking into consideration the characteristics of the patient (i.e. age) and the illness, is essential to pediatric practice.

Specific domain objectives:

• Obtain a focused history and physical exam and generate a differential diagnosis for a patients presenting with each of the following symptoms: Sore throat, difficulty swallowing, otalgia

• Describe the epidemiology, clinical presentation, evaluation, and management for each of the following diagnoses: Pharyngitis, strep throat, viral URI, herpangina, peritonsillar abscess, allergic rhinitis, otitis media, sinusitis, otitis externa
